Continuous and multi-verse playback for the Listen feature.

## What's new

### Listen continuously through a chapter
Hold **Shift** while clicking a verse's Listen button to start playback at that verse and roll through every following verse in the chapter automatically. The active verse's icon switches to a Stop square as it plays — clicking it (or pressing **Esc**) stops the whole queue. Plain (un-shifted) clicks still play just that one verse, same as before.

The chain stops at chapter boundaries. To keep going, navigate to the next chapter and Shift+click verse 1 (or wherever you want to resume).

### Play all selected verses
When you have verses selected (Click or Shift+Click multiple verses to build a selection), a floating action bar appears at the bottom of the window with a **Play selected** button. Clicking plays them in canonical reading order — book → chapter → verse — not selection order, so picking three verses out-of-order still produces a coherent listen-through. Works across books and chapters; the bar uses the same selection model the AI panel does.

While a multi-verse queue is active, the same bar shows a **Stop** button and a "Playing N of M" indicator so you can halt playback even if you've scrolled away from the current verse.

### Under the hood
The audio coordinator was refactored from "play a single WAV blob" to a lazy-synth queue: each verse's WAV bytes are only generated when its turn comes, not all upfront. A 30-verse chapter doesn't allocate 30MB of audio ahead of time. A transient TTS failure on one verse skips that verse rather than killing the whole chain. Pressing **Esc** still stops everything globally — the in-flight synth await sees the session token has been invalidated and bails on the next tick.

Listen buttons are also now hidden for original-language translations (WLC Hebrew, OSHB, SBLGNT Greek), since Piper's English voice produces nonsense on those.

## Upgrade

Standard. No re-download of Piper or the voice model needed.
